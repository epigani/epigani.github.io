My name is Emanuele Pigani. I am a PhD candidate in the School of Life, Health and Chemical Sciences at the Open University of London.  
I am a graduate student in Physics at the University of Padova, where I focused on topics in fundamental and applied non-equilibrium Statistical Mechanics.

I am also currently working under the supervion of Daniele Iudicone and within the [Laboratory of Interdisciplinary Physics](https://liphlab.com/){:target="_blank"}<!--_-->.

My main research interest focuses on the mathematical modelling of biological systems: in particular, I study consumer-resource models for explaining how critical properties (like coexistence) emerge in consumer-resource systems. From October 2021, I am delving in particular into the study of oceanic ecosystems.

More in general, I have a deep interest in understanding all the fundamental laws that underlie the reality. As Terenzio used to says,  "Homo sum, humani nihil a me alienum puto”.
